E-mail and notifications

Client: Thomson Reuters
Role: Research, UX, UI

Project

As time passed, the volume of emails and notifications sent by Thomson Reuters to its users continued to grow, resulting in a lack of consistency in both content and visual design. This project had two primary objectives.

Goals

Goal 1

Improve Content and Visual Patterns: The first goal was to enhance the consistency in the content and visual elements of the emails and notifications

Goal 2

Reduce User Communication: The second goal aimed to address the excessive number of messages being sent to users.

Stage 1

Understanding the Current Situation

The initial step to achieve our objectives was to engage with every team and compile a comprehensive list of all the emails sent by the platform at that time. This process allowed us to gain a complete overview of the emails sent and organize them by team and the stage in the user flow. This categorization helped us better understand the purpose and application of each email.

Analyzing Content

Upon gathering all the messages, a total of 87 messages were identified. To better comprehend their necessity, we conducted research involving stakeholders from each team. This enabled us to assess the importance and urgency of each message using the Eisenhower Matrix.

Analyzing Visual Design

While organizing messages based on their necessity, it became evident that the visuals lacked consistency. Each group of emails from different teams had distinct visual styles, which didn't follow a unified pattern.

Stage 2

Message Content Enhancement

In the second stage of the project, our primary focus was on removing unnecessary messages, as determined by the Eisenhower Matrix. The remaining messages underwent a revision, aligning them with the principles defined in a previously created UX writing document. Some messages required only minor modifications for simplification, while others were consolidated into single emails containing information from multiple sources.

Redesigning Email Templates

In this phase, our main objective was to identify and establish consistent visual patterns for email templates. We developed a set of standardized layouts to be used in various situations. Each layout was componentized and made available in Figma, streamlining the process for any designer to create new emails with flexibility.

Results

Achieved Objectives

The project's most significant accomplishment was the substantial reduction in the number of emails and notifications sent to users, effectively addressing a critical issue for users with varying levels of access to the platform.

Additional Metrics

As a testament to the impact of these changes, one team experienced a remarkable reduction from 87 notifications to 12 emails. Extrapolating this to a larger scale, with 299 companies, we estimated a reduction of approximately 349 emails sent in a month.

Final Considerations

In the journey of revamping the email and notification system for Thomson Reuters, several key takeaways and reflections have emerged from this project:

In closing, this project has been a significant milestone in improving the communication experience for Thomson Reuters users. By aligning content and visuals, streamlining messages, and promoting consistency, we've not only met our goals but also paved the way for a more user-friendly, efficient, and adaptable communication system.

User-Centric Design

Throughout this project, our unwavering focus was on enhancing the user experience. By reducing the volume of messages and creating a more consistent visual design, we aimed to make our users' interactions with the platform more intuitive and less overwhelming.

Collaborative Efforts

The successful outcome of this project would not have been possible without the collaboration of various teams and stakeholders. Engaging in discussions, gathering input, and working closely with each team allowed us to gain a comprehensive understanding of the email communication landscape.

The Power of Data

Data-driven decision-making played a pivotal role in shaping the project's direction. The use of metrics, such as the Eisenhower Matrix, enabled us to prioritize and refine our communication strategy effectively.

Impact on User Engagement

The reduction in emails and notifications directly addressed a long-standing user concern. As a result, user engagement and satisfaction levels have seen a noticeable improvement.

Continuous Improvement

The work does not end with the project's completion. To maintain the positive changes, it's crucial to keep monitoring and adjusting the communication strategy as user needs and platform requirements evolve.
